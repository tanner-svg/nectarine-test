Lauren Pinkston is running for Governor of Tennessee as an independent — a candidate who believes the state's citizens deserve better than a system that forces them to choose between two extremes. We were brought in to build an identity that could carry that conviction into every voter touchpoint.

## The Challenge

Political design has a grammar all its own: flags, eagles, red-white-and-blue, a candidate photo with a forced smile in front of a crowd. Lauren's campaign wanted none of it. The challenge was creating something that felt unmistakably like a campaign — but that stood apart from every visual trope voters have been trained to ignore.

## Strategy First

Before any design work began, we ran a strategy intensive with Lauren and her core team. The central finding: voters aren't tired of politics because of bad policies. They're tired of being talked at. Lauren's campaign is fundamentally about giving voters a seat at the table, and every piece of communication needed to reflect that.

We developed a messaging framework organized around a single idea: *Tennessee, Your Way.* Not a slogan — a governing principle that shaped tone, copy, and visual choices across the entire campaign.

## Identity and Web

The visual identity breaks with convention intentionally. A warm, human color palette — terracotta, linen, deep green — replaces the expected primary colors. Photography direction emphasizes genuine moments over staged tableaux. Typography is clean and confident without being corporate.

The campaign website we designed and built prioritizes voter stories over policy pages. The homepage leads with a question rather than a speech. The donation flow was rebuilt from scratch to reduce friction and increase conversion.

## Copywriting

We wrote the foundational copy across the website, email sequences, and printed materials — all in Lauren's voice, grounded in the messaging framework developed during strategy.
