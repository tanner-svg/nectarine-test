David Bruce spent two decades building a reputation as one of Nashville's most trusted independent financial advisors — but his brand looked like it had been assembled from a template in 2009. Prospective clients found a website that undersold his expertise before he'd said a single word.

## The Challenge

The brief was deceptively simple: make the brand match the man. In conversation, David is warm, direct, and disarmingly honest about money. His existing identity — generic serif wordmark, stock photography, boilerplate copy — communicated none of that.

## What We Built

We started with a half-day brand workshop to surface the positioning that was already there but hadn't been named. David's edge is that he talks to clients like a trusted friend who happens to know everything about their finances. We built the entire brand around that premise.

The visual identity uses a restrained palette of off-black and warm ivory with a single amber accent — sophisticated without being cold. The custom wordmark is set in a slightly humanist typeface that suggests approachability without sacrificing authority.

The website we designed and developed puts David's voice front and center, leading with his philosophy rather than his credentials. A custom intake form replaces the generic contact page, qualifying new clients before the first call.

## The Outcome

Within three months of launch, David reported that two of his highest-value clients that quarter had cited the website as the reason they reached out over a competitor.
