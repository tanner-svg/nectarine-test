The Fuzz Tax is Nectarine Studio's own publication and framework — a way of naming the invisible cost that fuzzy brands pay every single day. Unclear positioning, inconsistent visuals, copy that sounds like everyone else: each one is a small tax on growth that compounds over time.

## Origin

We kept having the same conversation with founders. They knew something was off with their brand but couldn't quantify it. Leads were harder to convert than they should be. Great hires chose competitors. Referrals didn't land the way they expected.

The problem wasn't their product. It was the fog around their brand — what we started calling *the fuzz*. We decided to build a vehicle for that idea that could reach founders before they became clients.

## The Work

The Fuzz Tax required us to turn the tools we use for clients on ourselves. We developed the visual identity, editorial voice, and content strategy as a complete system.

The brand leans into contrast: clinical data presentation paired with warm, direct language. Charts and frameworks that feel like they belong in a CFO's deck, written in prose that sounds like a conversation between friends.

**Brand identity** — wordmark, color system, grid, and icon language built to work across digital and print.

**Editorial framework** — a defined voice, topic taxonomy, and content cadence that any writer can plug into without losing consistency.

**Video production** — the brand's launch film, which distills the core idea in under two minutes and serves as the primary acquisition asset across paid and organic channels.

**Web** — a dedicated microsite housing the framework, the newsletter archive, and a diagnostic tool that lets founders estimate their own Fuzz Tax in real time.

## Why It Matters

The Fuzz Tax is a business case for clarity — and everything about the way it looks and reads has to prove that case. A fuzzy brand for a framework about fuzzy brands would be the loudest possible contradiction.
